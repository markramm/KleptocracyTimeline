Hurricane Katrina became the largest implementation of 'disaster capitalism' in U.S. history, with corporate interests using the crisis to advance privatization agendas previously blocked. Within 10 days of the levees breaking, .4 billion in no-bid contracts were awarded to four major contractors: Fluor (.4 billion), Shaw Group (50 million), Bechtel (75 million), and CH2M Hill (30 million). Halliburton/KBR received 24.9 million while government paid up to 29,000 per trailer costing 4,000-20,000 to manufacture.
