The IMF announces a $40 billion rescue package for Indonesia with unprecedented conditions: mandatory privatization of state-owned enterprises, elimination of subsidies, and comprehensive financial sector deregulation. These conditions effectively transfer economic control from local Indonesian elites to multinational corporations and Western financial institutions.
