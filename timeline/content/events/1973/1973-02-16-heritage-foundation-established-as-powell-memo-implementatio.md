The Heritage Foundation's founding represents a pivotal moment in conservative institutional infrastructure, directly implementing Lewis Powell's 1971 memorandum's strategy for coordinated corporate political influence. Established by Joseph Coors, Paul Weyrich, and Edwin Feulner, the organization pioneered an activist approach to policy research and implementation.
