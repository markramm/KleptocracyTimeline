Henry "Hank" Paulson confirmed unanimously by Senate as 74th Treasury Secretary after 32 years at Goldman Sachs, including role as Chairman and CEO. Required to liquidate over $600 million in Goldman Sachs stock holdings to comply with conflict-of-interest regulations. Total Goldman compensation estimated at $480 million over career, with $37 million in 2005 alone. Later conflicts emerge during 2008 financial crisis when his policies, including AIG bailout, directly benefit Goldman Sachs with estimated $12.9 billion windfall - the largest recipient of AIG public funds.
